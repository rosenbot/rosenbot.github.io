About

Born and raised in Salt Lake City, Noah has been a proud resident of District 5 his entire life. Noah attended West High School, before graduating from the University of Utah with a Bachelor of Arts in History. A member of Temple Har Shalom in Park City, Noah serves his community as a teacher in the temple's after school program, coordinator of the teen youth group, and mentor to the school's youth teaching assistants.

As an intern at the Utah State Legislature for the 2016 general session, Noah gained a unique perspective on government. In his time at the legislature, he was proud to help legalize needle exchange programs and expand access to the lifesaving drug Naloxone. He worked to find bi-partisan solutions to issues like the opioid epidemic currently ravaging our communities and the youth suicide crisis that touches the lives of far too many of our state’s children. As councilor, Noah will put to use the relationships and experience he got on the Hill, in order to advocate for the city he calls home.

As Director of Field Operations for Representative Carol Spackman Moss' 2016 re-election campaign, Noah has demonstrated his commitment to diversifying representation in Utah’s political sphere. He believes that Utah’s electorate should actively represent the citizens it serves; every voice matters and every vote counts. Furthermore, as a member of the LGBT community, Noah knows that diversity is key to balanced leadership. Noah has worked to give a platform to marginalized people in Utah, and will take a proactive approach to gender and sexuality diversity in a city with rich cultural history and a diverse, energized, and passionate population.

In Noah's current work for Valley Behavioral Health, he helps provide mental health and substance use disorder services to communities that are often left behind. As one of the leading causes of death in Utah and across the nation, he believes that substance abuse is an issue of public health, and intends to treat it as such.
